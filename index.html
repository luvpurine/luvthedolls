what do you mean by "transfeminine"?
transfeminine is a gender identity that falls under the transgender umbrella! while many may associate "transgender" with strictly MTF (male to female) or FTM (female to male), the umbrella term describes one whose gender identity is different from their assigned gender at birth. someone who is transfeminine is someone who falls under the transgender umbrella while being more feminine-leaning. for example, transgender women (main focus on this site), demigirls, or non-binary, genderfluid, and multigender individuals who are more feminine-leaning.
p.s. the term "dolls" are used to refer to transgender women!!
what is the purpose of this website?
i would like to increase appreciation for transfeminine girls. when i was researching voguing and ballroom culture for an essay, i saw how many dolls were shunned by their own families, subjected to violence, and even murdered for transitioning (i.e. Venus Xtravaganza and many transgender women in the voguing scene). even today, a large portion of the general population has harmful viewpoints towards those who are transfeminine specifically-- transgender women are getting killed. while violence is aimed towards all under the transgender umbrella, i've seen that a majority has been targeting transgender women (however, i do plan on making a similar site for transmasculine individuals). as of 2025, at least 325 people have been killed in such a fashion, and as of march 20, 2026, that number is at least 41.
am i transgender?
for the most part, that's something you'll have to figure out on your own. but one telling indicator is gender dysphoria, which is basically signals from the brain indicating that something is wrong (pertaining yourself). cisgender people can have gender dysphoria too (i.e. cisgirls feeling too masculine with body hair or cisboys feeling too feminine with gynecomastia). gender dysphoria can manifest as gender euphoria (feeling relief), body dysphoria, biochemical dysphoria, social dysphoria, societal dysphoria, sexual dysphoria, presentational dysphoria, existential dysphoria, and managed dysphoria.
how can i help?
honoring the lives of those who were lost, signing petitions against harmful legislature, speaking up, educating yourself, increase visibility of the dolls, and supporting some local LGBTQ+ visibility or anti-trans violence organizations is a start. find links to do so in the resources tab.
