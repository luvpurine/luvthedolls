fashion
Alex Consani (22)
American model signed with IMG, Model of the Year 2024, one of the first two transgender models to walk Victoria's Secret Fashion Show
Tracy Norman (73)
First mainstream African-American trans woman in modeling, Clairol "Born Beautiful" Campaign
Pia Davis
Founder of No Sesso, first transgender designer to present a fashion collection during Fashion Week (NYFW 2019)
Emira D'Spain (29)
Emirati-born American model, first Black transgender woman to model for Victoria's Secret
Valentina Sampaio (29)
Victoria Secret's and Sport's Illustrated Swimsuit Issue's first openly transgender model, one of the first two transgender models to walk Victoria's Secret Fashion Show
Enza Khoury (19)
Ohio teenager who has made her debut on the cover of I-D's "The Unknown Issue"
Te Manahou Mackay
Australian model signed with IMG, walked for many brands in Australian Fashion Week (i.e. Carla Zampatti)
Dara
Fashion stylist for celebrities like Hunter Scafer and Addison Rae under Second Name, signed with IMG Models, and the fashion director of Interview Magazine
Gogo Graham (34-35)
American fashion designer whose designs focus on the individual model's body and persona, advocate for issues transgender women face today
Munroe Bergdorf (38)
First transgender model in the UK for L'Oreal, "Changemaker of the Year" at the 2018 Cosmopolitan Awards
stem
Robin Isadora Brown
Earned her Ph.D. in developmental neuroscience at the University of Virginia. Her research ranges from glial cells in zebrafish to solar tracking sunflowers.
Janne Mesenholler
German psychologist whose work focuses on intersecting AI and human learning, has a masters in clincial psychology and is pursuing her PhD. Their research looks at AI as an educational tool and its ethical, legal, and social implications.
A.M. Kirsch
Neurophysiologist and writer of the novel Murder of an Uncommon Man, earned their PhD in neurobiology from USC, taught at UConnecticut and University of British Columbia, researched neuropathic pain and congestive heart failure, and member and former chair of the research Ethics Board
Ina Oestroem
Chemist doing research in renewable energy engineering, member of UNSW Hoex group, and her masters was focused on "the physical nature of non-covalent relations Supramolecular frameworks employing Density Functional Theory"
Tacey O'Neil
Mathematician and senior lecturer in analysis at the Open University. Her teaching focuses on pure mathematics while her research interests are geometric measure theory and fractals.
media
Cho Hyunju (played by Park Sung-hoon)
Also known as Player 120 in Squid Game, the popular South Korean survival thriller television series, Hyun-ju is a transgender woman who joined the games to pay for her gender-affirming surgery.
Lily Hoshikawa (va: Minami Tanaka, Zombieland Saga)
Lily Hoshikawa was a child prodigy and famous entertainer who, after being resurrected from death, becomes a member of the Saga's prefectures zombie idol group Franchouchou.
Tao Tachikawa (Con Cafe Jou wa Koi wo Jiru)
Tao Tachikawa is an employee at Runaway Cafe, a haute couture themed concept cafe, and an "aspiring" fashion designer.
athletics
Tifanny Abreu (41)
Brazilian volleyball player, first trans woman to play in Brazilian Women's Volleyball Superliga
Ana Caldas (47-49)
Portuguese-American swimmer who won first place in the U.S. Masters Spring National Championships (banned from swimming after)
Laurel Hubbard (48)
Weightlifter from New Zealand, first openly transgender woman in the Olympic Games, 7th internationally in +87kg
music
Ethel Cain (28)
American singer-songwriter and model who gained cult following after "Preacher's Daughter" debut album, 2024 Forbes 30 Under 30, over 3 million monthly listeners on Spotify. Her genres are gothic rock, Americana, indie rock, and ambient.
Anohni (54-55)
British singer who was the first openly transgender woman to be nominated for an Academy Award, album "I Am a Bird Now" won Mercury Prize. Her music is defined as experimental, electronic, dance, and art pop.
SOPHIE (died at 34)
Sophie Xeon was a British music producer, songwriter, and DJ who helped pave the way to 2010s hyperpop. She was nominated for the Grammy Award and worked with PC Music label artists as well as Charli XCX, Kim Petras, and Madonna. Unfortunately, Sophie passed away in 2021 after falling from a rooftop.
Jane Remover (22)
American rapper, producer, and singer-songwriter who identifies as non-binary. Their music falls under digicore, EDM, hyperpop, and shoegaze and they have over 800k monthly listeners on Spotify.
Shea Diamond (48)
American singer-songwriter and advocate for transgender rights. Her music falls under soul and R&B, and her song "I Am America" was featured in Billboard's list of best LGBTQ+ songs of 2020.
Wendy Carlos (86)
American musician, composer, and record producer who helped in the development and promotion of the Moog synthesizer, which won her three Grammys. Her unique synth sound led to her composing scores for The Shining, Tron, Beauty and the Beast, and A Clockwork Orange.
politics
Sarah McBride (35)
Congresswoman of Delaware's at-large district and former state senator whose legislation focuses on expanding access to health care, including mental health and media literacy programs in public schools, promoting sustainable tech, and protecting workers and families. McBride passed the Healthy Delaware Families Act and the Protect Medicaid Act.
Danica Roem (41)
Member of the Virginia Senate and journalist and the first openly transgender person to serve in a state senate in US History. Roem's policies focus on education, gun violence prevention, environmental care, and health care.
Brianna Titone
Member of the Colorado House of Representatives from the 27th district and scientist. Titone's policies focus on protecting workers, consumers, and small businesses.
Kim Coco Iwamoto (57)
Member of the Hawaii House of Representatives and a fourth generation Japanese-American. Iwamoto's policies focus on the protection of LGBTQ+, healthcare and food security for all, humane immigration reform, sustainable tourism, and a safe climate.
influencers
Vivian Wilson, @vivllainous (22)
American social media influencer and model who is the eldest daughter of El*n M*sk and his first wife Justine. She is politically left and supports human rights, universal basic income, health care, and reducing the wealth gap. Currently she has over 1 million Instagram followers and almost 2 million TikTok followers, Wilson is also signed with CAA labels and a prominent feature on Teen Vogue.
Freddie Chen (24)
American content creator who has over 2 million followers whose content focuses on makeup, LGBTQ+ culture and rights, comedy, and lifestyle. She is also the co-host of the T4T Podcast
Ilya Selyn (30)
Tiktok influencer with over 2.5 million followers, Selyn's content focuses on makeup, fashion, and beauty to promote diversity, self-acceptance, and equality.
Mathlida Hogberg (31)
Swedish social media influencer who has over 200k followers whose content focuses on travel, beauty, and her experiences as a transgender woman.
screen and stage
Hunter Schafer (27)
American actress and activist who's known for her role as Jules in Euphoria, joined lawsuit against North Carolina's Public Facilities Privacy and Security Act, and was named to Teen Vogue's 21 under 21 list.
Janet Mock (42-43)
American televison producer and writer who's known for work on televison show Pose (which casted actually trans actresses and depicted queer culture accurately), contributing editor for the Marie Claire magazine
MJ Rodriguez (35)
Michaela Jae Rodriguez is an American actress and singer known for her lead role on televison show Pose, making her the first transgender woman be nominated for an Emmy award in a major acting category and to her winning the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress in a Television Series Drama
Lana Wachowski (60)
American film and televison director, writer, and producer who works alongside her sister Lilly, best known for directing the Matrix (1999)
Lilly Wachowski (58)
American film and televison director, writer, and producer who works alongside her sister Lana, best known for directing the Matrix (1999)
Indya Moore (31)
American actor and model who identifies as non-binary. They're best known for playing Angel Evangelista in Pose, and they were named TIME's top 100 most influential people in the world in 2019.
Zion Moreno (31)
American actress who is best known for her role in Gossip Girl (2021) as Luna La
Josie Totah (23-24)
American actress (who is of Palestinian and Lebanese ancestry) known for her recurring role in Disney Channel's Jessie and as Lexi in the 2020 revival of Saved By the Bell
Tommy Dorfman
American actress who's best known for playing Ryan Shaver in 13 Reasons Why (2017)
Laverne Cox (53)
American actress and LGBTQ advocate who's best known for her role in Orange is the New Black, which made her the first transgender person to be nominated for an acting Primetime Emmy Award and the first openly transgender person to appear on the cover of TIME, Cosmopolitan, and have a wax figure of herself at Madame Tussauds.
Eva Reign
American actress and journalist who's known for her lead role in Anything's Possible (2022) and has written for Vogue, New York, Them, and more.
Juliana Joel
American actress who's known for her role as Nikki in Raven's Home
